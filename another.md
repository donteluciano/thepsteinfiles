# Epstein Files — Media & Source Coverage Directory

*Every media outlet, podcast, independent journalist, and documentary that has produced significant Epstein coverage. Organized by tier based on volume and impact. Article counts are estimates based on archive searches and are approximate.*

*This list serves two purposes: (1) source material for the encyclopedia's articles/citations, and (2) potential content for the site's "Resource" sections.*

---

## TIER 1: BREAKTHROUGH / DEFINITIVE COVERAGE
*These outlets broke major stories, shifted the narrative, or produced the most comprehensive ongoing coverage.*

### Miami Herald (~200+ articles)
- **Key reporter:** Julie K. Brown
- **Landmark work:** "Perversion of Justice" series (November 2018) — the investigation that single-handedly revived the Epstein case, leading to his 2019 re-arrest. Brown tracked down dozens of victims and exposed the 2008 NPA failures. Won two George Polk Awards. Brown's book *Perversion of Justice* (2021). Continues active coverage of 2026 DOJ releases. Alan Dershowitz tried to pressure Pulitzer committee to exclude Brown.
- **Status:** Still actively covering, though relying on some wire copy for volume.

### Bloomberg News (~150+ articles)
- **Key work:** Independently obtained 18,700 emails from Epstein's personal Yahoo account (September 2025) — cryptographically and forensically verified. This email cache is the single largest independent acquisition of Epstein documents outside the DOJ releases. Their financial reporting on Leon Black, Ariane de Rothschild, and the banking connections has been particularly strong. Merrie Spaeth (Epstein's PR coach) story broke here.
- **Status:** Major ongoing coverage, especially financial angles.

### New York Times (~300+ articles)
- **Key reporters:** Multiple investigative team members
- **Key stories:** Leon Black / $158M payments to Epstein. Steven Mnuchin flying on Milken's plane. Extensive coverage of Maxwell trial. Bill Gates-Epstein meetings. Harvard/MIT funding scandals. Vanity Fair profile suppression (via Vicky Ward). Broad and deep institutional coverage since 2019.
- **Criticism:** Employed Landon Thomas Jr., who privately corresponded with Epstein while covering financial figures. Slow to cover Israel intelligence angle.
- **Status:** Ongoing major coverage.

### Wall Street Journal (~200+ articles)
- **Key stories:** Leon Black's financial architecture revealed from Epstein files (69 bank accounts, $2B art collection as collateral). Trump's alleged "bawdy" 50th birthday note to Epstein (2003). Extensive corporate accountability coverage. Owned by Rupert Murdoch's News Corp (Murdoch himself in Epstein's black book).
- **Status:** Ongoing, especially corporate/financial angles.

### Washington Post (~200+ articles)
- **Key stories:** Broad political coverage, especially Trump administration handling of files. Milken pardon reporting. Congressional pressure for release. CREW FOIA requests on Mnuchin.
- **Status:** Ongoing.

### The Guardian (~250+ articles)
- **Key stories:** Prince Andrew coverage (UK angle). John Casablancas/Elite Models connection. Peter Mandelson revelations. Amelia Gentleman's "patriarchy in action" analysis of the emails. Crown Princess Mette-Marit. UK political fallout (Starmer government). Morgan McSweeney resignation.
- **Notable quote:** "The emails showcase the private behaviour of a male ruling class."
- **Status:** Ongoing, strongest on UK/European angles.

### Associated Press (~100+ articles)
- **Key work:** Part of the 5-outlet collaboration reviewing 3.5M DOJ pages (with CBS, NBC, MS NOW, and CNBC). FBI investigation analysis — found proof of sexual abuse but little evidence of trafficking ring. Systematic review of DOJ internal records.
- **Status:** Ongoing collaborative review.

### Reuters (~100+ articles)
- **General coverage:** Wire service providing foundational reporting distributed to hundreds of outlets worldwide. Maxwell investigation and arrest coverage.
- **Status:** Ongoing.

## TIER 3: INDEPENDENT / INVESTIGATIVE OUTLETS
*Smaller outlets doing outsized, original investigative work — often covering angles mainstream media won't touch.*

### Drop Site News (~30+ articles)
- **Key reporters:** Ryan Grim, Murtaza Hussain, Jeremy Scahill
- **Key work:** Exclusive series on Epstein's connections to Israeli intelligence (September-November 2025). Described by The Nation's Jeet Heer as "blockbuster reporting...not matched by any comparable investigations in The New York Times or The Washington Post, on CNN or the major networks." Israeli intelligence angle is their signature contribution.
- **Status:** Ongoing, leading independent investigation.

### Zeteo (Mehdi Hasan) (~20+ articles/episodes)
- **Key work:** Mehdi Hasan's ongoing coverage after his MSNBC show was cancelled. "Unshocked" series with Naomi Klein. "Ask the Editor" live shows. Rep. Ro Khanna interview on cover-up allegations. Edward Luce interview. Among the most persistent independent voices on the story.
- **Status:** Very active.

### Narativ (~15+ articles/investigations)
- **Key reporters:** Zev Shalev
- **Key work:** Reported the Milken-Epstein connection — specifically the unconfirmed claim that Epstein worked at Drexel Burnham Lambert alongside Leon Black in the 1980s. This claim hasn't been confirmed by mainstream outlets but connects key dots.
- **Status:** Ongoing.

### Democracy Now! (~20+ segments)
- **Key work:** Amy Goodman's coverage. Victim-centered reporting. Early attention to the story.
- **Status:** Ongoing.

### The Intercept (~15+ articles)
- **Key reporters:** Ryan Grim (before moving to Drop Site)
- **Key work:** Early reporting on Epstein connections to intelligence agencies.
- **Status:** Occasional.

### Columbia Journalism Review (~10+ articles)

### Al Jazeera (~30+ articles)

### Middle East Eye (~15+ articles)

- **Status:** Ongoing, focused on Israel angle.

### Mondoweiss (~10+ articles)
- **Key work:** Epstein-Israel connections coverage alongside Drop Site and Al Jazeera.

### TRT World (~10+ articles)

### The Hill (~50+ articles)

### Politico (~40+ articles)
